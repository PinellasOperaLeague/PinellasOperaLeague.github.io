Letter from our President

Dear members of the Pinellas Opera League

During the four years that Christina Alexander served as president of the POL, the league forged community relationships with the public library system that now includes a popular free opera video program available to the public. Thanks to the video donations by the league, the Seminole Community Library at SPC now has the distinction of having the largest opera video collection in the area. The POL hosted a master class at Ruth Eckerd Hall and participated in the Great American Teach-In and the Adopt-a-School program in the Pinellas county schools. Increases in revenues generated through fund raisers allowed the league to award a Pinellas Opera League Scholarship as a community partner with the St. Petersburg College Foundation. Also during Christina’s tenure, the POL website has been updated and a POL Facebook page was inaugurated.

We wish Christina well in her new endeavor with the Education Department while working on her Doctorate in Humanities.

Volunteers Needed!

We are looking for volunteers to help Marcia Lork with the “Great American Teach-In” and to participate in outreach programs in our schools and the community. At the ”Great American Teach-In” on November18, 2015, Vice President Marcia Lork spoke to six classes about opera at Pinellas Central Elementary – the adopted school of the POL – and lived to tell about it! Marcia hopes that in the future others will join her to enlighten children about the beauty of opera.

Publications

New member Ron Regan has joined our board as Chairman of the Publications Committee. Ron has set us up on Facebook with the goal of increasing membership. Ron has also worked with other organizations posting pictures and events on Facebook which he will do for us.
