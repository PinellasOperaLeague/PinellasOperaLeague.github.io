Susan Hellman Spatafora - Guest Performer

Members bring a guest and have a great time. All are welcome!

Susan Hellman Spatafora has performed with companies such as Palm Beach Opera, St. Petersburg Opera, Sarasota Opera, Glimmerglass Opera, and Central City Opera. Her featured roles have included Susannah in Carlisle Floyd’s Susannah, Micaëla in Carmen, Mimì in La Bohème, Clotilde in Norma, and Giannetta in L’Elisir d'Amore. She has been a prize winner in competitions including the Florida Grand Opera Young Patronesses of the Opera competition, the Palm Beach Opera International Vocal Competition, and the National Society of Arts and Letters Voice Competition. Upcoming engagements in the 2015-2016 season include appearances with the Tampa Oratorio Society in Mozart’s Mass in C Minor and Dvorak’s Stabat Mater, as well as concerts with IlluminArts in Miami, St. Petersburg Opera and the Tampa Bay Symphony.

Join us!

Luncheon meetings are a fun way to socialize and meet others who enjoy opera and music. On the first Wednesday of each month, October to June, the League sponsors a luncheon at the Dunedin Country Club. 1050 Palm Blvd., Dunedin, FL 34698 (727) 733-2134.

The cost for members is $22 and for non-members $25. You can pay at the door with exact cash or a check made out to the Pinellas Opera League.

The luncheon meetings begin at 11:00 am with a time for socializing. Business 11:30, program 11:45 and lunch 12:30.

Reservations must be made by Friday evening October 2, and cancellations by Saturday morning October 3. Contact Nancy @727-738-4007. Please be sure to call in your reservations on time.

The League is obligated to pay for all reserved luncheons. No shows and reservations not cancelled by the above deadline will be billed.

Luncheon Sponsors

If you sponsor our luncheon, you get to sit at the performers’ table and chat with the artists. Please contact Nancy Jewitt at 727-738-4007. Sponsorship money is used to help pay for the performers’ meals. Thanks to Margarita and Peter Garcia who have donated $220.00 to cover the artists’ luncheons for the rest of the year.
